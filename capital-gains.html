Individual assessee guide

Capital gains tax needs clean records, correct classification, and timely planning.

A practical overview for resident individual taxpayers dealing with sale of equity shares, mutual funds, immovable property, gold, or other capital assets.

Quick view

Common capital gains points clients should track.

Indicative general information as of May 2026. Capital gains tax can vary by asset class, holding period, acquisition date, residency, treaty position, exemptions, and notifications.

Indicative capital gains treatment for individuals

Asset / gain type Typical tax treatment Useful note
Listed equity shares / equity-oriented mutual funds - STCG under section 111A Special rate generally applies Check STT condition, date of transfer, and current applicable rate before filing.
Listed equity shares / equity-oriented mutual funds - LTCG under section 112A Tax applies on gains above the specified annual threshold Schedule 112A details may be required in the ITR for eligible securities.
Immovable property May be short-term or long-term depending on holding period and law applicable to year Stamp duty value, cost, improvement cost, transfer expenses, and exemption planning matter.
Gold, jewellery, debt funds, and other assets Treatment depends on asset type, acquisition date, and holding period Maintain purchase bills, sale records, bank trail, and valuation support.
Inherited or gifted assets Cost and holding period may refer to previous owner rules Document inheritance, gift deed, will, probate, and original acquisition data.

Documents normally required

Purchase deed / contract notes Sale deed / redemption statement Broker capital gain report Bank statements for payment trail Improvement bills and invoices Valuation report, where relevant Stamp duty valuation details Exemption investment proof

Planning points before selling an asset

Before sale

  • Confirm holding period and classification.
  • Estimate tax before finalising cash flow.
  • Check whether losses can be set off.
  • Review advance tax impact.

After sale

  • Collect complete evidence immediately.
  • Track exemption deadlines carefully.
  • Reconcile AIS, broker reports, and books.
  • Select the correct ITR form and schedule.
